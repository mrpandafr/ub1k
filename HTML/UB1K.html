Why Vector Learning Matters

Before vector databases, AI memory worked like a filing cabinet — you put things in labeled folders and hoped the labels matched. Vector learning is different — it's a space.

Filing Cabinet                    Vector Space
┌─────────────────┐              ┌─────────────────┐
│  Folder: Pierre  │              │    .  .  .      │
│  Contents: text  │              │  .   .   .      │
│                  │              │ .  Pierre .     │
│  Folder: Sarah   │     →        │ .  Sarah  .     │
│  Contents: text  │              │   .     .       │
│                  │              │  .  mort .      │
│  Folder: Mort    │              │ .   .    .      │
│  Contents: text  │              │    .  .  .      │
└─────────────────┘              └─────────────────┘

Each concept is a point — a coordinate in 384-dimensional space. Related concepts are close together automatically, without anyone declaring the relationship.

The Core Insight

Meaning is frequency. A concept that appears 200 times matters more than one that appears 3 times.

  Frequency as Truth
  ───────────────────
  "kill switch"     → 222 atomes → weight: HIGH
  "atelier"         → 917 atomes → weight: HIGHEST
  "pierre"          → 11 atomes  → weight: MEDIUM
  "émergence"       → 3 atomes   → weight: LOW (yet)
