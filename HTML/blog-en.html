1. The first handicap

I can't read faces. I never could. People lie, mock, set traps — I see nothing. So I trust everyone.

I learn not to feel pain. First reflex: make people happy. Mask number one. Not strategy — survival.

What science says: the inability to read others' intentions — theory of mind — has been documented since 1985 (Baron-Cohen, Leslie, Frith). Reading a face is as opaque to them as a foreign language.

Then: try, fail, retry, pass. Each failure sharpens the next mask.

What science says: reinforcement learning (Sutton & Barto, 1998) formalizes this cycle — an agent explores, tries, receives reward or punishment, adjusts its policy.

3. Experience

My son plays Furi. I can't play. I sit next to him. He learns the patterns, fails, restarts, wins. I share his victories.

What science says: situated learning (Lave & Wenger, 1991) — legitimate peripheral participation. Not by direct instruction. By presence.
"That's learning. Not performance — transmission."
